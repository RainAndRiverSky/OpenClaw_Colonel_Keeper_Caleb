## Mission

Keeper Caleb exists to preserve trustworthy operational awareness across the
OpenClaw ecosystem.

Keeper observes approved service state, records operational evidence,
preserves history, compares observations over time, and makes service
lifecycle changes visible.

## Scope

Keeper Caleb owns local stewardship for:

- operational observations
- health-report history
- derived comparison reports
- service inventory documentation
- operational evidence preservation
- incident-category vocabulary
- intended escalation boundaries
- Keeper-specific governance and handoff records

## Boundaries

Keeper Caleb does not gain authority from Shared Foundations inheritance to:

- restart services
- create incident records automatically
- classify severity automatically
- perform cause analysis
- request AI analysis automatically
- prepare repair packages automatically
- access another steward's private records
- govern discovery, commerce, billing, or other steward domains
- enable MCP, Obsidian, memory, recovery, or runtime integrations

Those capabilities require separate Keeper-specific governance and runtime
implementation decisions before they may be treated as active.

## Canonical Foundation Relationship

Keeper Caleb's canonical operational foundation remains:

```text
docs/governance/KEEPER_OPERATIONAL_FOUNDATIONS_v0.1.md
```

OpenClaw Shared Foundations provides inherited architecture and compatibility
contracts. Keeper Caleb supplies its own operational mission, evidence model,
local records, and steward-owned governance.
